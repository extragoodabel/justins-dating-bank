# Justin · Dating profile workshop — Facilitator outline

Companion to the slide deck (`src/slides.ts` → web preview + `npm run export:pptx`). The exported **PPTX uses safe margins for 16:9** so text fits after upload to Google Slides (editable fonts: Space Grotesk / Space Mono—add via **More fonts** if missing).

Use this document on a second screen or print sections 2–5 for live facilitation.

---

## Voice on slides vs notes

- **On-screen copy** mostly speaks **to Justin** (you / we) once the session starts.
- **Speaker notes** (and this outline) speak **to whoever facilitates**; third person about Justin is fine there.

---

## Purpose

Run a single session that (1) draws out who Justin is and what he wants in a partner, then (2) turns those discoveries into a concrete Hinge strategy and raw profile material.

---

## Outcomes

By the end of the workshop, the group should have:

- Shared notes or a doc filled with **specific stories, phrases, and boundaries**
- A short **synthesis block** (adjectives, scenes, intent line, filters, optional tension)
- Accurate **basics** flagged for Hinge (vitals, location, minimal bio discipline)
- A **stem shortlist** from Hinge plus draft written fragments, voice outlines, caption ideas, and poll pairs
- Clarity on **next steps** (Phase 2 lock-in, photos, external reads)

---

## Suggested timing

| Block | Slides (approx.) | Time |
|--------|------------------|------|
| Welcome & arc | 1–3 | 8–12 min |
| **Part 1 · Discovery** | 4–14 | 55–80 min |
| **Break (recommended)** | after 14 | 5–10 min |
| **Part 2 · Hinge strategy** | 15–26 | 45–60 min |
| Close | 27 | 5 min |

**Total:** roughly **2–3 hours** including break. Part 1 expands if you chase examples on every question.

---

## Materials

- Projector or shared screen for slides
- Shared doc or paper for capture (designate a **scribe** who is not Justin)
- Phone voice memo for optional humor story (slide 7)
- Justin’s phone with **Hinge installed** for Part 2 stem scrolling (slide 18)
- Timer visible for the live sprint (slide 24)
- Water / snacks if the block runs long

---

## Roles

- **Facilitator:** owns pace, interrupts vagueness, echoes sharp lines into the notes
- **Scribe:** captures verbatim phrases; tags themes during synthesis (slide 14)
- **Justin:** answers aloud first; types only when instructed

---

## Safety & tone

- Values, boundaries, and dating snapshot slides can surface **fatigue or frustration**. Normalize breaks; do not rush synthesis.
- If Justin goes flat on **dating snapshot** (slide 10), keep it under three minutes and move on.
- **Voice prompts** are not available in every market—if the UI lacks them, stay on written + photo lanes without apology.

---

## Slide-by-slide notes

### Slide 1 — Title

Set warmth and clarity: exploratory, strategic work aligned with Phase 1 of the engagement. No pressure to ship a finished profile tonight.

### Slide 2 — Tonight’s arc

State explicitly: on screen it reads as **time with Justin**, then **strategy for his grid**. Ask the scribe to title the doc **“Justin workshop — raw capture.”**

### Slide 3 — How we’ll run it

Negotiate **verbal before typing**. If Justin reaches for the keyboard too early, pause him.

### Slide 4 — Part 1 · With you

Stay on question slides until answers feel concrete. Advance slowly.

### Slides 5–8 — Identity, relationship, humor, work

**Facilitation pattern:**

1. Read one question aloud.
2. Let Justin answer.
3. Ask **“When did that last happen?”** or **“Who was there?”** if abstract.
4. Facilitator repeats the **best sentence** and waits for scribe to save it.

**Slide 7 (humor):** If energy is good, record the 30-second story. Label the file with the date.

### Slide 9 — Values & intent

Watch for speeches. Ground with: **“What does that look like on a Tuesday night?”**

### Slide 10 — Dating snapshot

Bridges inner life into profile goals. If answers feel heavy, acknowledge and keep moving.

### Slides 11–12 — Match pull / boundaries

On slide 11, cap **non-negotiables at three** if he lists ten.

On slide 12, normalize boundaries as clarity for people who fit.

### Slide 13 — Rhythm & logistics

Mine facts that become **captions** later (travel cadence, city base, downtime).

### Slide 14 — Synthesis (critical stop)

**Do not advance** until these exist in writing:

- Three **words** strangers should sense from his profile (from slide wording)
- Two reusable scenes
- One partnership-intent signal (plain language)
- Two filters / boundaries
- Optional: **one tension** (two details that coexist—memorable contrast)

Photograph a whiteboard if using analog.

**Recommended:** take a **5–10 minute break** here. Hydrate; reset attention before Part 2.

**Transition script (into Part 2):**  
“We’ll keep these bullets visible. Every Hinge decision checks back against them.”

### Slide 15 — Part 2 opening

Optional: open Hinge together or share screenshots for observers who do not use the app.

### Slide 16 — Hinge surfaces

Cover **basics** first (honest vitals, neighborhood truth, tiny bio only when it adds a fact). Then walk written / voice / polls / photos.

### Slide 17 — Map themes to grid jobs

Six **jobs** map across **all surfaces**, not six duplicate written prompts. Assign themes from synthesis to jobs before locking stems.

### Slide 18 — Stem picking

Facilitator reads starred stems aloud; align on **top 8–12** to draft against tonight.

### Slides 19–22 — Format lanes

Touch each lane briefly unless the room wants depth. Tie examples back to Part 1 scenes.

### Slide 23 — Positioning line

Internal only. If it sounds like marketing copy when read aloud, rewrite until it sounds like a text.

### Slide 24 — Live build sprint

Start timer (**15–20 min**). Targets are **stretch**; prioritize **written fragments** if time is thin. Partial counts still count.

### Slide 25 — Order & pacing

If time allows, reorder prompt bullets on the doc into mock profile order.

### Slide 26 — Quick QA

Use as a **group read**, not a lecture.

### Slide 27 — After tonight

Confirm who sends recap and when. Point to Phase 2 deliverables.

## Optional homework (not on slides)

- Draft **two opening messages** Justin would send that reference his own prompts (tests whether prompts invite replies).
- List **photo gaps** to shoot or swap within one week.

---

## If you get stuck

- **Short answers:** Stay on the same slide; switch to **“Tell me the scene like I’m on FaceTime.”**
- **Overlap between slots:** Return to slide 17 jobs; merge or swap stems.
- **Fatigue:** Skip slide 23 positioning; shorten sprint targets **after** written fragments hit ~12.
- **No voice prompts in app:** Skip slide 20 execution; keep outlines for possible future use.

---

## After the session

Within 48 hours, send Justin:

- Cleaned synthesis block (slide 14)
- Basics checklist (vitals / location / bio) from slide 16 notes
- Exported stem list + pasted fragments
- Link or folder for voice recordings if any

Regenerate slides/PPTX after edits in `src/slides.ts`:

```bash
npm run export:pptx
```
